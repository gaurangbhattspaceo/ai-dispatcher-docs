# Jobs

The Jobs page is your dispatch queue — it shows all jobs imported from your FSM platform, their readiness for dispatch, and the results of AI optimization.

Navigate to **Dashboard → Jobs** to open the dispatch queue.

## Queue Tabs

Jobs are organized into three tabs:

| Tab | What It Shows |
|-----|--------------|
| **Queue** | Jobs synced from your FSM that are ready (or pending readiness) for dispatch |
| **AI Dispatched** | Jobs sent to the AI optimizer — shows assigned technician and timing |
| **Confirmed** | Jobs with accepted AI suggestions that have been written back to your FSM |

Each tab shows a live count of jobs in parentheses next to its label.

## Syncing Jobs

Click **Sync from [FSM name]** to pull the latest jobs and technicians from your connected platform. The button label automatically shows your connected FSM (e.g., **Sync from ServiceTrade**, **Sync from Jobber**).

After a sync, a confirmation banner appears showing:
- Total jobs imported
- How many are **Ready**, **Warnings**, and **Not Ready**
- Any sync errors

## Type Filter

Use the filter bar above the job table to filter by job type:

| Filter | Shows |
|--------|-------|
| **All** | Every job in the current tab |
| **One-off** | Single-occurrence jobs |
| **Recurring** | Repeating jobs with multiple scheduled visits |

Recurring jobs show a **Repeat** icon and expand to reveal individual visit rows when clicked. One-off jobs show a **Calendar** icon.

## Technician Readiness Summary

After every sync, a collapsible **Technician Readiness** panel appears below the job list. It shows how many of your technicians passed all dispatch pre-checks. Click the row to expand and see individual technician issues — such as missing skills, no availability set, or address not geocoded.

<Tip>
Fix technician issues in your FSM or in **Settings → Technicians**, then re-sync to update their readiness status.
</Tip>

## Readiness Filter

Use the filter bar above the job table to narrow by readiness state:

| Filter | Shows |
|--------|-------|
| **All** | Every job in the current tab |
| **Ready** | Jobs that passed all pre-dispatch checks |
| **Warnings** | Jobs with non-blocking issues (e.g., missing optional fields) |
| **Issues** | Jobs that are blocked from dispatch due to missing required data |

## Batch Dispatch

<Steps>
  <Step title="Select jobs">
    On the **Queue** tab, check the boxes next to the jobs you want to dispatch. Only **Ready** and **Warnings** jobs can be selected — **Issues** jobs are excluded until their data is fixed. Use the header checkbox to select all selectable jobs at once.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Send to optimizer">
    Click the **Dispatch [N] Jobs** button that appears in the header. If no individual jobs are selected, the button shows **Dispatch All Ready ([N])** — which sends every ready job in one batch.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm">
    A banner confirms how many jobs were dispatched and tells you results will appear in the **AI Dispatched** tab when the optimizer finishes.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review suggestions">
    Switch to the **AI Dispatch** page to review the optimization results and accept or reject each assignment.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
Jobs flagged as **Ready** have passed all pre-dispatch checks. Jobs marked **Issues** are missing required data (e.g., no matching skill or unresolved address). Fix the underlying issue in your FSM and re-sync to make them ready. **Warnings** jobs can still be dispatched but may produce lower-quality suggestions.
</Tip>

## Expanding Readiness Issues

Any job row with a readiness warning or issue shows a chevron (▶) next to its badge. Click the row to expand an inline issues panel showing:

- **Which field** has the problem (e.g., `requiredSkills`, `latitude`)
- **What the issue is** (plain-language description)
- **What to do** — a recommended action to resolve it

Click the row again to collapse the panel.

## AI Dispatched Tab

Once jobs are sent to the optimizer, they appear in the **AI Dispatched** tab. Each row shows:

- Job title and customer details
- The technician the AI assigned (shown in the **Assigned To** column)
- Estimated arrival and departure time
- Dispatch timestamp

For jobs created from a public booking link, a badge may appear below the status column:
- **Customer Confirmed** (green) — the customer confirmed their preferred time slot
- **Awaiting Customer** (amber) — the customer hasn't confirmed yet; the slot is tentatively held

<Note>
The **Assigned To** column shows **Pending...** while the optimizer is still working. Refresh the tab after a few seconds to see the assigned technician.
</Note>

## Job Details

Each job row includes:
- Customer name and service address
- Required skills and job type
- Time window (scheduled date/time)
- Service duration
- Current readiness badge
- Priority badge (Emergency or Urgent, when applicable)
- Time since last sync

## Pagination

The job table shows up to 50 jobs per page. Use the **Previous** and **Next** buttons at the bottom to navigate. A count above the buttons shows how many jobs you're viewing out of the total.

## Job Status Badges

| Badge | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| **Ready** | All required data is present; job can be dispatched |
| **[N] warnings** | Non-critical issues; job can still be dispatched |
| **[N] issues** | Missing required data — cannot dispatch until resolved |
| **Pending** | Readiness check not yet completed |
| **Processing** | Job is being optimized by the AI |
| **Suggested** | AI has made an assignment suggestion — review and accept or reject |
| **No Match Found** | AI could not find an available technician with the required skills |
| **Rejected** | You rejected the AI's suggestion |
| **Processing Error** | Something went wrong during optimization |
| **Scheduled** | Visit has been confirmed and written back to your FSM |
| **Cancelled** | Visit was cancelled |

## Priority Badges

| Badge | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| **Emergency** | Highest priority — urgent response required |
| **Urgent** | Elevated priority — should be dispatched before standard jobs |

## AI Dispatched Tab — Visit Actions

When you expand a job in the **AI Dispatched** tab, each scheduled visit appears as a separate row with its own action buttons:

| Button | When It Appears | What It Does |
|--------|----------------|--------------|
| **Accept** (green) | Visit has an AI suggestion ready | Locks in the assignment and writes it to your FSM |
| **Reject** (red) | Visit has an AI suggestion ready | Opens a reason dialog — select why you're rejecting, then the visit is unassigned |
| **Resync** (blue) | Visit was previously rejected, unassigned, or failed | Re-sends the visit to the AI optimizer for a new suggestion |
| **Cancel** (red outline) | Visit is in Suggested or Scheduled state | Removes the assignment — technician is unassigned, slot is freed |

For **recurring jobs**, expanding the job row reveals all scheduled visits (e.g., "JOBNBR-V1", "JOBNBR-V2"). Each visit can be accepted, rejected, resynced, or cancelled independently.

If a visit was reshuffled by the optimizer, it shows a **Reshuffled** badge.

## Batch Actions in AI Dispatched Tab

When you select one or more job rows in the **AI Dispatched** tab, the action bar changes to show three buttons:

- **Accept (N)** — Accept all AI suggestions for the selected jobs at once. Writes all assignments back to your FSM in a single operation.
- **Reject (N)** — Reject all suggestions for the selected jobs. Opens a confirmation dialog before processing.
- **Resync** — Re-send all selected jobs to the optimizer (useful after fixing an issue in your FSM).

The batch action bar shows how many jobs are currently selected, so you know exactly what will be affected.

After you run a batch action, a toast notification reports the outcome: how many succeeded, how many are awaiting customer confirmation, how many were skipped (no active suggestion), and how many failed.

<Note>
Jobs with no AI suggestion yet (still Processing) cannot be accepted or rejected — wait for the optimizer to finish, then refresh the tab.
</Note>
