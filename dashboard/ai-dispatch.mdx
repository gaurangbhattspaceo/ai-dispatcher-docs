# AI Dispatch

The AI Dispatch page is where you review, approve, and manage AI-generated job assignments. The optimizer analyzes every pending job and technician simultaneously to produce the most efficient daily schedule.

Navigate to **Dashboard → AI Dispatch** to open the dispatch board.

## How It Works

<Steps>
  <Step title="Jobs enter the queue">
    Pending jobs are pulled from your scheduling software (or uploaded via CSV). Each job is checked for dispatch readiness — valid address, required skills, time window.
  </Step>
  <Step title="AI runs optimization">
    The optimizer considers technician skills, current location, availability, travel time, workload balance, and customer preferences to produce ranked assignment suggestions.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review suggestions">
    Each suggestion appears with the recommended technician, confidence score, estimated arrival, and AI reasoning. You can accept, reject, or reassign.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Accepted assignments sync">
    Accepted suggestions are written back to your connected scheduling software automatically — no manual entry needed.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Assist Mode vs Auto Mode

At the top of the page, you'll see two mode buttons: **Assist Mode** and **Auto Mode**.

<Frame caption="The Assist Mode / Auto Mode toggle at the top of the AI Dispatch page">
  <img src="/images/screenshots/ai-dispatch-auto-mode.png" alt="Mode toggle between Assist and Auto" />
</Frame>

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Assist Mode">
    **Assist Mode** puts you in control. The AI waits for you to click **Generate Schedule** before running optimization. Use this when you want to:
    - Plan your whole day at once each morning
    - Make manual changes before running the optimizer
    - Review everything before any assignments go out
    
    This is the default mode and works well for most dispatch managers.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Auto Mode">
    **Auto Mode** keeps the schedule continuously optimized throughout the day. Once enabled, the AI monitors for new jobs, cancellations, and schedule changes — then automatically re-runs optimization when it detects something has changed.
    
    When Auto Mode is active, a floating **Auto Mode Monitor** widget appears in the bottom-right corner of the page. It shows you:
    - 🟢 **Monitoring** — watching for changes
    - 🟡 **Processing** — currently re-optimizing
    - ⚫ **Idle** — paused or outside business hours
    - **Last Run** and **Next Run** times
    - A live activity log of every change detected and re-optimization triggered

    Use Auto Mode for high-volume days when jobs come in throughout the day and you need the schedule to stay optimal without constant manual runs.
    
    <Note>Auto Mode only runs during business hours. Outside your business hours, it pauses automatically and resumes the next morning.</Note>
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Reviewing Suggestions

Each AI suggestion card shows:
- **Recommended technician** — name, skill match, current utilization
- **Confidence score** — how strongly the optimizer prefers this assignment
- **Arrival & departure times** — estimated based on current location and travel
- **Alternative technicians** — other viable options with their scores
- **Conflict warnings** — any overlapping jobs or time window violations
- **AI reasoning** — plain-language explanation of why this technician was chosen

### Action Buttons on Each Row

Every suggestion row has a small set of action icons:

| Icon | When It Appears | What It Does |
|------|----------------|--------------|
| ✅ **Approve** (green checkmark) | Job is ready to confirm | Accepts the assignment and writes it back to your scheduling software |
| 📞 **Call Customer** (phone) | Job needs your confirmation | Opens the Call Customer dialog so you can confirm the appointment directly with the customer |
| 🔄 **Reassign** (circular arrows) | Always visible | Opens the Reassignment dialog to pick a different technician |
| ⚠️ **Resolve Conflicts** (orange triangle) | Job has scheduling conflicts | Opens the Conflict Resolver |
| ⋮ **More Actions** (three dots) | Always visible | Extra options like "View Full Details" and "Approve Anyway" |

### Calling a Customer to Confirm

Some jobs are marked **Needs Review** — meaning the AI recommends the assignment but wants you to confirm it with the customer before locking it in. These jobs show the **phone icon** (📞) instead of the green checkmark.

Click the phone icon to open the **Call Customer** dialog. Inside you'll find:

- **Call Script** — a ready-to-read script with the customer's name, appointment time, technician name, and job type. Just read it out loud — no preparation needed.
- **Call Outcome** — three buttons to log what happened:

| Outcome | What to click | What happens |
|---------|--------------|--------------|
| Customer confirmed the time | **Confirmed** (green) | Assignment is accepted and syncs to your scheduling software |
| Customer wants a different time | **Reschedule** (yellow) | Flags the job for manual rescheduling |
| No answer | **No Answer** (gray) | Closes the dialog — you can try again later |

<Tip>
You can also call a customer for any assignment (even ones that don't require review) by clicking the **⋮ More Actions** menu and choosing **Call Customer**.
</Tip>

## Batch Actions

Select multiple suggestions using the checkboxes and use the **Batch Action Bar** to:
- Accept all selected assignments at once
- Reject a group of suggestions
- Reassign a batch to a specific technician

## Unassigned Jobs Panel

After generating a schedule, if some jobs could not be assigned to any technician, an **Unassigned Jobs** panel appears in the Dashboard view below the Batch Action Bar. Each unassigned job shows:

- **Job number and customer** — so you can identify the job quickly
- **Why it wasn't assigned** — a plain-language explanation (e.g., "No technician with the required skills available in the time window")
- **Possible reasons** — additional contributing factors
- **Recommended actions** — concrete steps to resolve the issue
- **Severity badge** — Critical (red), Warning (amber), or Info (blue)

<Note>
The Unassigned Jobs panel only appears in the **Dashboard** view and only when the schedule has at least one unassigned job. If all jobs were successfully assigned, the panel is hidden.
</Note>

Address unassigned jobs by resolving the underlying issue in your FSM (adding a required skill to a technician, extending their availability, or adjusting the job's time window), then click **Generate Schedule** again.

## Conflict Resolution

When the optimizer detects a conflict (overlapping jobs, unavailable technician, time window violation), a **Conflict** badge appears on the affected card. Click it to open the **Conflict Resolver**, which shows the exact issue and suggests corrective actions.

## Emergency Jobs

Use the **Emergency Job** button (🚨) to insert an urgent job into the current schedule. The optimizer will re-plan around it and show you which existing assignments need to shift, with an impact summary before you confirm.

## Unavailable Periods

Mark technicians as unavailable for specific time blocks (lunch, training, PTO) using the **Unavailable Periods Manager**. These blocks are respected during optimization — jobs are never assigned during marked unavailable periods.

## AI Reasoning Panel

Click the **Reasoning** button on any suggestion to open a detailed explanation of the optimizer's decision, including:
- Which rules were evaluated
- Why alternative technicians ranked lower
- Specific scores for travel time, skill match, and workload

## Common Questions

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="What's the difference between Assist Mode and Auto Mode?">
    In Assist Mode, you click "Generate Schedule" whenever you want the AI to run. In Auto Mode, the AI watches for schedule changes throughout the day and re-optimizes automatically. Most teams start with Assist Mode and switch to Auto Mode once they're comfortable with the AI's suggestions.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Can I use CSV upload without a FieldCamp connection?">
    Yes! Select **CSV Upload** as your data source and upload your jobs and technicians files. Download the templates from the upload cards to make sure your CSV columns match what the optimizer expects.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="What does the Auto Mode Monitor widget show?">
    The floating widget in the bottom-right corner shows whether Auto Mode is actively monitoring (green dot), currently re-optimizing (yellow), or idle (gray). It also shows when the last run happened, when the next one is scheduled, and a log of recent activity like "Change detected — 2 new jobs added" or "Re-optimization completed — 8 assignments updated."
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Will Auto Mode dispatch jobs without me reviewing them?">
    Auto Mode re-runs the optimization and updates the suggestions on screen. You still review and accept assignments. The only exception is if you've enabled automatic acceptance in your AI settings — but that's off by default.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="What if I get an error generating the schedule?">
    Check that your jobs have valid addresses and time windows, and that your technicians have their skills and availability set up. The error message at the top of the page will tell you what's missing.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="What does 'Needs Review' mean on a job?">
    When the AI is confident about the assignment but wants a human to confirm it first — for example, when the customer's preferred time is tight or the technician is at maximum capacity — it marks the job as **Needs Review**. You'll see a phone icon instead of the green checkmark. Click it to call the customer and confirm, then mark the outcome as Confirmed, Reschedule, or No Answer.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="What happens if I close the Call Customer dialog without clicking a button?">
    Nothing changes. The job stays in Needs Review status and you can come back to it later. No assignment is made until you click Confirmed.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
