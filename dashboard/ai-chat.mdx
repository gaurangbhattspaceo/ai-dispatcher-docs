# AI Chat

The AI Chat page gives you a conversational interface to your dispatch plan. Ask plain-English questions about your schedule, get constraint explanations, and request optimization changes — without leaving the dashboard.

Navigate to **Dashboard → AI Chat** to open the chat window.

## Getting Started

When you first open AI Chat with no active conversation, the page shows a **"What can I help with?"** welcome screen with four ready-made suggestion cards:

| Card | What It Does |
|---|---|
| 👷 **Who's working today?** | Lists all technicians scheduled for the current day |
| 📋 **What jobs are unassigned?** | Shows every job not yet assigned to a technician |
| 📅 **Show the full schedule** | Gives a plain-English summary of today's full plan |
| 🔄 **What if David is sick tomorrow?** | Runs a what-if scenario on technician availability |

Click any card to send that question instantly — no typing needed.

## Chat Controls

Two controls appear in the top header bar and input area:

| Control | Where | What It Does |
|---|---|---|
| **+ New chat** (top right) | Header | Clears the current conversation and starts fresh. Disabled while the AI is responding. |
| **■ Stop** (input bar, right side) | Input | Stops the AI mid-response. The partial reply stays on screen. Only visible while the AI is generating. |

Press **Enter** to send a message. Use **Shift + Enter** for a newline without sending.

## What You Can Ask

The AI assistant has direct access to your live schedule and optimization engine. Some things you can do:

- **Understand the plan:** "Why was John not assigned any jobs today?"
- **Check conflicts:** "Which jobs have overlapping time windows?"
- **Analyze workload:** "Who has the most driving today?"
- **Request changes:** "Move the 2 PM appointment to someone closer to downtown."
- **Explain scores:** "Why does this assignment have a low confidence score?"

## What Happens While the AI Thinks

While the AI works on your question, you'll see a typing indicator (three animated dots) and a small status label showing exactly what it's doing in real time:

| Status Label | What It Means |
|---|---|
| 🔧 Checking schedule... | Reading your current schedule, visits, or solution summary |
| 🔧 Preparing changes... | Building a plan modification based on your request |
| 🔧 Confirming update... | Writing the accepted change back to the plan |
| 🔧 Analyzing constraints... | Examining optimization scores, rule violations, or conflicts |
| 🔧 Checking feasibility... | Testing whether a requested change is actually possible |
| 🔧 Processing... | General computation in progress |

These status labels disappear once the AI has finished each step and is ready to reply.

## If Something Goes Wrong

When the AI can't respond, it shows an amber info card in the chat with a bold error title, actionable bullet points explaining what to do, and — for temporary errors — a **Try Again** button you can click to resend your message without retyping it.

| Error Title | What It Means | What to Do |
|---|---|---|
| **Scheduling Service Unavailable** | The AI service is under heavy load or temporarily down | Click **Try Again**, or wait a minute and retry |
| **Scheduling Service Unavailable** (maintenance) | Scheduled maintenance is in progress | Try again in a little while |
| **Request Timed Out** | Your question took too long to process | Click **Try Again**, or break it into a shorter question |
| **Too Many Requests** | You've sent a lot of messages quickly | Wait 30 seconds, then click **Try Again** |
| **Session Expired** | Your login session ended | Refresh the page and log in again |
| **AI Assistant Not Configured** | AI Chat isn't enabled for your organization | Contact your administrator |
| **AI Assistant Offline** | The AI service is unreachable | Contact your system administrator |
| **Connection Lost** | Network connection was interrupted | Check your connection, then click **Try Again** |

<Note>
The **Try Again** button only appears on temporary, retryable errors. For non-retryable errors (session expired, not configured, offline), follow the "What to Do" guidance above instead.
</Note>

## How It Works

The AI assistant uses the same optimization engine that powers the AI Dispatch page. When you ask it to make a change, it:

1. Reads the current schedule and constraints
2. Evaluates whether the change is feasible
3. Shows you a preview of the impact
4. Applies the change only after you confirm

<Note>
The AI Chat assistant does **not** automatically write changes to your FSM. Changes made through chat update the AI Dispatcher plan; you still review and accept them on the AI Dispatch page before they sync back.
</Note>

## Message Actions

Hover over any completed assistant message to reveal two small icon buttons below the text:

| Button | What It Does |
|---|---|
| **Copy** (📋 icon) | Copies the full message text to your clipboard. The icon briefly turns into a checkmark (✓) to confirm. |
| **Retry** (↺ icon) | Re-sends your last question from scratch if you want a fresh response. Only appears on the most recent assistant reply. |

<Note>
The Retry hover button re-runs the last question without any changes. If you want to ask a slightly different question, just type a new message instead.
</Note>

## Tips for Best Results

- Be specific: "Move job #1234 to Sarah" works better than "reassign that job"
- Ask one thing at a time for complex changes
- If the AI says a change isn't feasible, ask it why — it can explain the exact constraint blocking it
- Use the **AI Dispatch** page for bulk approvals; use **AI Chat** for investigation and one-off adjustments
