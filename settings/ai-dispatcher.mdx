# AI Dispatcher Settings

Fine-tune how the AI optimization engine assigns jobs to your technicians. Settings are saved per organization and apply to every planning run.

Navigate to **Settings → AI Dispatcher** to access these options.

## Optimization Profile (Presets)

Choose a preset to instantly apply a balanced configuration for your business type. Selecting a preset updates all sliders automatically.

| Preset | Best For |
|--------|----------|
| **Balanced** | Most businesses — equal weight on all priorities |
| **Minimize Travel** | Reducing fuel costs and drive time between jobs |
| **Fill All Jobs** | Ensuring every job gets assigned, even at the cost of longer routes |
| **Balance Workload** | Distributing jobs evenly across all technicians |
| **Custom** | Fine-tune individual weights yourself |

<Tip>
Start with **Balanced** and only switch presets if you notice a specific problem — too much driving, uneven technician workloads, or jobs going unassigned.
</Tip>

## Optimization Sliders

When you select **Custom** (or manually adjust any slider), you control four independent priorities. Each slider runs from **1 (Low)** to **10 (High)**.

### Job Assignment Priority

How hard the AI tries to assign every pending job. At higher values the optimizer accepts longer routes and imperfect schedules to avoid leaving jobs unassigned.

- **Low (1–3):** Accepts some unassigned jobs in exchange for tighter, more efficient routes
- **High (7–10):** Pushes hard to assign everything, even if travel time increases

### Time Window Strictness

How precisely the AI honors customer arrival windows. At higher values, showing up within the promised window outweighs other factors like travel efficiency.

- **Low:** Flexible — the AI can deviate from windows to improve overall efficiency
- **High:** Strict — arrival windows are treated as hard constraints

### Travel Optimization

How strongly the AI minimizes driving distance between jobs.

- **Low:** Travel time is a minor factor; other priorities dominate
- **High:** Shortest routes are heavily rewarded; technicians stay close to home base

### Workload Balance

How evenly jobs are distributed across your technician team.

- **Low:** The best-fit technician always wins, even if their day is already full
- **High:** Jobs are spread evenly; no one technician gets overloaded

## Preferred Technician Matching

Controls how strongly the AI favors assigning a job to a customer's previously-assigned technician (when that history is available in your FSM).

| Level | Effect |
|-------|--------|
| **Off** | No preference — purely optimized by availability and skill |
| **Low** | Slight nudge toward the familiar technician |
| **Medium** | Moderate preference — familiar tech wins ties *(default)* |
| **High** | Strong preference — familiar tech is chosen unless genuinely unavailable |

<Note>
Preferred technician data comes from your connected FSM platform. If your FSM does not track previous assignments, this setting has no effect.
</Note>

## Business Hours

Also on this page: **Company Business Hours** — set the default working hours for each day of the week. These are used when individual technicians do not have their own schedules configured.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Enable working days">
    Check the box next to each day your team works.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set start and end times">
    Enter the start and end time for each active day using the time pickers.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save">
    Click **Save Business Hours**. Changes take effect on the next planning run.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Saving Settings

- **Save Optimization Settings** — saves your preset/slider/preferred-tech choices
- **Reset Defaults** — restores the **Balanced** preset with default slider positions
- Settings are stored per organization and persist across sessions
